Blitz Box

Roll. Match. Shut.

A dice and tiles game where every roll leads to a decision with real consequences. Three game modes, 18 unique cards, and centuries of game design — reimagined.

The Game

Blitz Box is a modern take on Shut the Box, an ancient dice game that's been played in pubs, on ships, and across kitchen tables for centuries. The rules are simple: roll the dice, close tiles that match the total, and try to shut every tile on the board. But simple rules don't mean simple decisions.

We discovered the original game at a Goodwill store for $4 and couldn't stop playing. So we built something new — bold, fast, and packed with more ways to play.

Three Worlds, One Box

Each game mode lives in its own visual world with distinct rules, aesthetics, and level of complexity.

Classic

The Pub

The original game. One row of tiles numbered 1-9. Roll two dice, close any combination matching the total. When only tiles 6 and under remain, switch to one die. Pure and timeless.

1 row No cards Warm & classic

Two Rows

The Casino

Front row and back row. Back tiles are blocked by the front — you have to clear a path before you can reach them. Sequencing matters. Planning ahead is everything.

2 rows No cards Strategic depth

Triple Stack

The Arcade

Three rows of tiles with a full card economy. Draft powerups at the start, earn more by rolling doubles or clearing rows. But watch out — bad surprise cards can reopen tiles, freeze your board, or strip your hand.

3 rows 18 cards Full chaos

The Cards

In Triple Stack mode, cards change everything. Nine powerups you can play from your hand, and nine bad surprises that trigger when you least expect them.

Powerups

Play these anytime from your hand to turn the tide.

Wild Card Replace any tile's value with any number 1-9
Copycat Copy a closed tile's value to another row and close it
Lucky Seven Set the roll to exactly 7
Triple Down Next roll uses 3 dice instead of 2
Blitz! Close all open tiles in one chosen row instantly
Echo Replay your previous roll total
Sniper Close any single open tile — no roll required
Shelter Absorb the next bad surprise with no effect
Swap Trade values of two open tiles in different rows

Bad Surprises

Drawn from the deck when you roll 2 or 12, hit a pair, or trigger a force event. You don't choose these — they choose you.

The Shakedown Discard a random powerup from your hand
The Jammer No powerups for 2 turns
Tile Plague Reopen 1 random closed tile
Gravity Shift Shuffle all open tile values randomly
Triple Tile Plague Reopen up to 3 closed tiles
Lock Out Freeze a random open tile for this turn
Debt Collector Reopen the highest-value closed tile in current row
Chain Break Closing a tile reopens its neighbors
Dead End Permanently close the lowest open tile in a row

The History

Shut the Box has been played for over a century. The earliest written record dates to 1893 in France, where it was known as Jeu du Trac. It spread sailor-to-sailor across ports and coastlines, became a staple in British pubs by the 1950s, and even appeared on NBC's High Rollers game show in 1974.

There's something about the game that just works — simple enough to explain to anyone, tense enough to make every roll matter. Blitz Box takes that foundation and builds on it with blocking mechanics, a card economy, and three distinct visual worlds.
