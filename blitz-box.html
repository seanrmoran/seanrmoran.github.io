Blitz Box

Roll. Match. Shut.

A dice and tiles game where every roll leads to a decision with real consequences. Three game modes, 18 unique cards, and centuries of game design, reimagined.

The Game

Blitz Box is a modern take on Shut the Box, an ancient dice game that's been played in pubs, on ships, and across kitchen tables for centuries. The rules are simple: roll the dice, close tiles that match the total, and try to shut every tile on the board. But simple rules don't mean simple decisions.

We discovered the original game at a Goodwill store for $4 and couldn't stop playing. So we built something new: bold, fast, and packed with more ways to play.

Three Worlds, One Box

Each game mode lives in its own visual world with distinct rules, aesthetics, and level of complexity.

Classic

The Pub

The original game. One row of tiles numbered 1-9. Roll two dice, close any combination matching the total. When only tiles 6 and under remain, switch to one die. Pure and timeless.

1 row No cards Warm & classic

Two Rows

The Casino

Front row and back row. Back tiles are blocked by the front, so you have to clear a path before you can reach them. Sequencing matters. Planning ahead is everything.

2 rows No cards Strategic depth

Triple Stack

The Arcade

Three rows of tiles with a full card economy. Draft powerups at the start, earn more by rolling doubles or clearing rows. But watch out: bad surprise cards can reopen tiles, freeze your board, or strip your hand.

3 rows 18 cards Full chaos

The Cards

In Triple Stack mode, cards change everything. Nine powerups you can play from your hand, and nine bad surprises that trigger when you least expect them.

Powerups

Play these anytime from your hand to turn the tide.

Wild Card Replace any tile's value
Copycat Copy a closed tile to another row
Lucky Seven Set your roll to 7
Triple Down Roll three dice
Blitz! Close all tiles in a row
Echo Replay your last roll
Sniper Close one open tile
Shelter Block the next bad surprise
Swap Trade a tile between rows

Bad Surprises

Drawn from the deck when you roll 2 or 12, hit a pair, or trigger a force event. You don't choose these. They choose you.

The Shakedown Discard 1 random powerup
Gravity Shift Shuffle open tile values
Lock Out Freeze one tile this turn
Tile Plague Reopen 1 tile
Triple Tile Plague Reopen 3 tiles
Dead End Close the lowest tile
Debt Collector Reopen a high-value tile
The Jammer No powerups for 2 turns
Chain Break Reopens a tile next close

The History

The origins of Shut the Box are murky, and probably better for it. Some claim the game was invented by Norman fishermen in the 12th century, played on the lids of old sea chests to gamble for the day's catch. Others say it was a sailors' game, passed from port to port on merchant routes across the English Channel. None of this is documented, but the stories feel right for a game that has always lived in taverns, on docks, and around kitchen tables.

What is documented starts in 1893, when the game appeared in French records as Jeu du Trac. From there it spread through the Channel Islands and into British pub culture, where it became a staple by the 1950s. Cannonball, Bakeratz, Jackpot, Klackers: the game picked up names everywhere it went. It even showed up on American television when NBC's High Rollers game show featured it on July 1, 1974.

We found our copy at a Goodwill in Sonoma County for $4. We couldn't stop playing, so we built Blitz Box.
